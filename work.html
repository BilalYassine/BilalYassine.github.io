 AiDTR Student Programmer 

I am currently working as a student programmer for a U.S. Army Aided Target Recognition (AiDTR) Project through the Data Science Institute at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I am working to integrate Robot Operating Systems (ROS), Unity 3D Game Engine, and Microsoft's AirSim in order to simulate and test robot vehicles within a virtual physics environment. My role on the team has included C#/C++/Python scripting, building Universal Robotic Description Format (URDF) files, and integrating ROS within Unity 3D environments.

The skills I have learned or refined through my experience on the AiDTR team include C++, C#, Python, Unity, ROS, Robot Development, Socket Programming, and Git.
